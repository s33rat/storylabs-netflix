The Open Frame — identity concept
Visual Identity Direction

The screen
becomes a world.

The Open Frame is the visual argument of the brand. Three sides close — authority, cinema, containment. One side opens — expansion, world, invitation. The incomplete rectangle is not a flaw. It is the identity.

01 Three closed sides carry Netflix's cinematic authority and the contained power of a story well told.
02 The open right edge — activated in red — is the StoryLab signal: the story is escaping into world.
03 The frame draws in real time because the world is not given. Someone has to make it. StoryLab makes it.
04 ✕ Without the open edge, there is no story world. The closed frame belongs to cinema. The open frame belongs to StoryLab.
05 Clearspace equals the full height of the mark on all sides. The frame must always breathe.

Why We Exist — mission, vision, purpose, values
Creates worlds,
not content.
Netflix StoryLab creates and expands cinematic stories into immersive, cross-format narrative worlds. Our mission is to transform single titles into enduring cultural ecosystems — to become the global home of story worlds that audiences explore, shape, and belong to.
From passive
to participatory.
To transform storytelling from passive consumption into a shared cultural experience — where the end of an episode is the beginning of a deeper relationship with the story. Unlike platforms built on volume, we create deep narrative ecosystems for cultural impact and long-term audience belonging.
Value 01
Immersion Over Interruption
Every format choice deepens engagement rather than fragmenting it. The world pulls you in, not past you.
Value 02
Story Depth Over Content Overload
One great world with genuine depth is worth more than a hundred titles with nowhere to go.
Value 03
Global Reach, Local Roots
The most resonant stories are specific. Cultural particularity is the gateway to universal feeling.
Value 04
Creative Bravery
We take creative risks on the stories that deserve them. Excellence is non-negotiable. Safety is not.
"The greatest stories were never contained by a single format. We exist for the ones that were always meant to be worlds — and the creators willing to build them."
